# The AI infrastructure reckoning: Optimizing compute strategy in the age of inference economics
### march - stock market trend analysis MySQL project 
**date:** 08-03-2026  
**topic:** AI infrastructure and inference economonics

### summary
Companies are eager to use AI, but struggle with unprecendented costs of compute power. This is a massive shift from training models that students/employees use to models that manage the "inference of economics" (using AI daily for projects/ enterprise-level engines using a lot of data).

### key takeaways
* **cloud importance**: AI cannot exist without robust cloud infrastructure. It's easy to build an AI tool, but difficult to make one in such a way that 10,000 employees can use without the company spending so much on cloud computing. AWS/Azure
* **ethics and environment**: high electricity consumption and ethical concerns such as geopolitical issues like wars are major "headwinds" (unfavourable market developments that hinder a company's growth). Companies would need specialized chips and efficient code to keep costs down.
* **ESG(Environmental, Social and Governance)**: Large companies now need to report their carbon footprint, so if their AI uses too much eletricity, their stock prices could go down.

### connection to my SQL project
This article highlights why data optimization (cleaning and structing data to maximize efficiency) matters. If I can write efficient SQL queries that use less compute time (power and time), I am helping a company save money on their cloud bill.
